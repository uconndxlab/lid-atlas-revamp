About the LID Atlas

The National Low Impact Development (LID) Atlas was created by the NEMO Program at the University of Connecticut's Center for Land Use Education and Research (CLEAR) to highlight innovative stormwater practices around the country. These practices are commonly referred to as either low impact development (LID) or green stormwater infrastructure. The goal of the site is to encourage and educate local officials and others about low impact development practices by providing specific, local examples of their use.

This is not intended as an inventory of all existing practices, but rather is a portal for the discovery of what these practices look like, where they have been installed, and how they are working. Each project balloon contains project specifics, a summary of the project, photos (when available) and links to more information. The mobile version of the Atlas allows anyone with a Google or Twitter login to add projects to the Atlas and also includes size of practice and size of drainage area data.

The full version of the National LID Atlas built by John Ray, David Dickson, Mark Hensel, Kate Woodruff, Timothy Lawrence, and John Rozum. The mobile version of the Atlas was designed by David Dickson and Michael Dietz, built by Yaira Rivera, and funded through a competitive grant from the United States Department of Agriculture/National Institute of Food and Agriculture , project #CONS2013-05768.

For information on embedding a localized version of the Atlas on your website, please contact david.dickson@uconn.edu.
